# HR_Analytics_Dashboard
This HR Analytics project aims to analyze workforce data to identify demographic patterns, departmental trends and the key drivers behind employee attrition
## üìå Project Overview
Employee attrition is a significant challenge that impacts recruitment costs, productivity, and team morale.
This project leverages **Power BI** to analyze workforce data, identifying the key drivers behind employee turnover and providing HR leaders with actionable, data-backed insights to improve retention strategies.

## üìä Business Questions Addressed
* What is the total headcount and the current active employee count?
* Which departments and job roles are most affected by attrition?
* How does the company's attrition rate compare to the industry benchmark (12%)?
* What is the demographic breakdown (Age, Gender, Ethnicity, Marital Status) of the workforce?
* What specific factors (Travel, Overtime, Tenure) contribute most to employees leaving?
* How have hiring and attrition trends evolved over time?

## üí° Strategic Recommendations

1.  **Sales Department Intervention**: Conduct targeted job satisfaction surveys and review workload distribution to stabilize the Sales team.
2.  **Travel Policy Reform**: Introduce flexible travel policies or rotation schedules for roles requiring frequent movement to prevent burnout.
3.  **Onboarding & Mentorship**: To combat high attrition in the 0-2 year tenure bracket, implement a formal mentorship program and clear growth pathways.
4.  **Overtime Monitoring**: Audit departments with consistent overtime to determine if headcount needs to be increased to protect work-life balance.
5.  **Salary Equity Review**: Perform a deep-dive compensation audit across ethnic groups to standardize pay frameworks and ensure fairness.
